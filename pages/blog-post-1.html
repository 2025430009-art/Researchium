Linear Algebra: The Core of Modern Computing

By Divyanshu Ranjan | March 2026

Linear Algebra is not just about solving for $x$. It is the mathematical language behind 3D graphics, Artificial Intelligence, and the research I do in Video Coding (HEVC/VVC).

1. The Concept of Vector Spaces

At the heart of Linear Algebra lies the Vector Space. A vector space $V$ over a field $F$ is a set of objects (vectors) that can be added together and multiplied by scalars. For it to be a valid space, it must satisfy the 8 axioms, including commutativity and associativity.

Key Formula: The linear combination of vectors $v_1, v_2, \dots, v_n$ is given by: $$V = c_1v_1 + c_2v_2 + \dots + c_nv_n$$

2. Matrices and Transformations

In our B.Sc curriculum, we focus heavily on matrices. A matrix isn't just a grid of numbers; it's a Linear Transformation. When you multiply a vector by a matrix, you are rotating, scaling, or shearing that vector in space.

3. Why This Matters for Research

If you are interested in Discrete Cosine Transform (DCT) architectures for video compression, you are essentially performing matrix multiplications to transform spatial data into frequency data. This reduces the size of video files without losing quality.
